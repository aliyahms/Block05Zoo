Zoo Animals

Bears

Bears are large, four-legged mammals with stocky legs, broad heads, and short tails. They have shaggy coats, small rounded ears, and plantigrade paws with five nonretractile claws. Bears have a variety of colors, including black, brown, cinnamon, white, blue-gray, and cream. Their size varies by species, but they all have massive frames, heavy paws, and powerful claws. Bears are carnivorans, or meat eaters, but they also eat a lot of plants. Their diet includes roots, seeds, berries, nuts, insects, fish, and deer. They have long, strong canines for holding and killing prey, and wide, bumpy molars for grinding vegetables. Bears have poor eyesight but a great sense of smell, and their long snouts indicate their highly evolved sense of smell. Bears are found on every continent except Antarctica, in a wide variety of habitats from Arctic ice packs to tropical forests. They are good at climbing trees and swimming, and in the fall, they often go to large rivers for salmon and to riparian forests for roots, berries, and fruits. In the winter, most brown bears hibernate in alpine and subalpine areas, while most black bears hibernate in forests. During hibernation, bears can go for up to 100 days without eating, drinking, urinating, or defecating, living off a layer of fat they build up in the summer and fall. Female bears will often rouse during hibernation to give birth to up to four cubs.

Giraffes

Giraffes are the tallest land animals in the world, with males reaching up to 18 ft tall and weighing up to 3,000 lbs. They are known for their long necks, which can be up to 6 ft long and contain seven vertebrae, just like humans, but with elongated bones. Giraffes also have long eyelashes and legs, and are covered in a unique brown and white spotted coat.

Lions

Lions (Panthera leo) are large cats native to Africa and India with muscular, broad-chested bodies, short, rounded heads, and round ears. They have powerful forelegs, teeth, and jaws for killing prey, and their coats are yellow-gold. Adult male lions are larger than females and have shaggy manes that can be blond, reddish-brown, or black, depending on age, genetics, and hormones. The length and color of a lion's mane is a sign of strength and vitality, and is also used for protection during fights. Young lions have light spotting on their coats that disappears as they grow.

Monkeys

Monkeys are tailed primates that are furry and live in warm regions. They are social and intelligent animals that are distinguished from apes by their narrow chests and tails, even if they are just small nubs. Most species are arboreal, using all four limbs to leap from tree to tree. They can sit upright and stand erect. Most species run along branches rather than swinging arm over arm like the apes. Monkeys are highly social omnivores, organized in clans as large as several hundred individuals headed by an old male.

Alligators

Alligators have a long, rounded snout that has upward facing nostrils at the end; this allows breathing to occur while the rest of the body is underwater. The young have bright yellow stripes on the tail; adults have dark stripes on the tail. It's easy to distinguish an alligator from a crocodile by the teeth.
